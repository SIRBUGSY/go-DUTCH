Your tutor

I have been teaching languages since 2010 and help students not just learn Dutch, but use it confidently in everyday life, work, study and communication in the Netherlands.

My connection with the Netherlands began long before I started teaching. For more than 15 years I worked as an international journalist, travelled to the Netherlands frequently, conducted interviews for business publications and saw first-hand how the language sounds in a real professional setting. I later lived in the Netherlands for 5 years, so I understand not only the language but life in the country itself: conversations with neighbours, letters from the Gemeente, paperwork, work, daily routines and cultural nuances.

My approach combines communicative methodology and the lexical approach. This helps students gradually start thinking in the language rather than translating every word in their head. We study grammar through context too — clearly and practically.

My legal background helps me explain administrative and business language: letters, contracts, documents, KvK and official formulations. And if you need Business English or IELTS preparation, I work with those as well.

FAQ

How long is a lesson?

A standard lesson lasts 60 minutes. For intensive exam preparation, 90-minute sessions are possible; the price is agreed individually.

Are lessons online or in person?

All lessons take place online via Zoom. This allows us to use interactive whiteboards, record sessions for review and study at any convenient time from anywhere in the world.

How do I cancel or reschedule a lesson?

Cancellation or rescheduling without losing payment is possible no later than 24 hours before the lesson starts. Cancellations with less than 24 hours' notice are charged in full.

How can I pay?

Payment is accepted by direct bank transfer to an IBAN (European account) and via SBP (for Russian bank cards).

Do I need to buy textbooks?

No purchases needed. All notes, audio, tests and homework are provided digitally via Google Drive. A purchase is only necessary if you personally prefer a physical book, or if your specific goal requires specialist material not yet in my library.
